Background:

We had a task where workers looked at some text and find words that are parts of proper nouns such as people name, organization names and places.

Now we want to know whether you can predict if a worker miss a word, after looking at labels by that worker.

Worker summary:

You will first look at a summary of the labels by a particular worker. You will see the sentences and the proper nouns in bold and whether the worker the worker miss each proper noun. For example:

Word Miss
It’s official: Obama attending APEC Summit in Manila
 YES

Where APEC is an organization name and should be marked but the worker missed that. The sentences by that workers are given in Tables . Each table includes sentences that are similar. For example:

Majority features: All characters are uppercases;  

In this table, you see three examples of sentences and the words that the worker missed. You also see that the table has 50 rows (3 are shown, 47 are not shown). These 50 rows correspond to 30% of the words that the worker missed. Lastly, you see the Majority feature: all characters are uppercases, meaning that the majority of sentences in this table have the bold word with that feature. This suggests that the worker here is not good at labeling all-uppercase words.

Notes:

A few tables are empty (you can ignore those). Some Majority features are also empty.

Task:

You will then answer some question. Each question contains two words; worker gave the correct labels for one of those and the wrong label for the other. Your task is to guess which word did the worker miss. For examples:

1.  New York  Metropolitan Opera suspends conductor James Levine

2.  One dead after hit-and-run driver strikes six in Queens, NY

To help us understand your answer, please select an explantion (or write your own explanation).

For example, if you believe the worker is not good at all-uppercase words, you should select 2 and select the explanation "worker bad at all uppercase (acronym)". In contrast, if you believe the worker is good at all-uppercase words, you should select 1 and select the explanation "worker good at all uppercase (acronym)"

If you have absolutely no clue, you can select the option "Can't tell" and select the explanation "Cant' tell". But if you have any clue, you should not select this.
